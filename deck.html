How It Works

A closed loop where low-temperature heat compresses a liquid that spins a turbine.

High-velocity gas Low-velocity water High-velocity mixture High-pressure liquid Low-pressure liquid Refrigerant return Energy in / out
1
Evaporation
Low-grade waste heat vaporizes a refrigerant into a high-velocity gas.
2
Mixing
The gas is injected into a water stream, transferring its momentum and condensing.
3
Pressurization
A diffuser decelerates the flow, converting kinetic energy into static pressure.
4
Power generation
The high-pressure mixture drives a hydroelectric turbine that produces electricity.
5
Separation
Water and refrigerant split and recirculate — closing the loop with no fuel.
